Hi there!

Welcome to my personal website! My name is Laura Koemmpel, and I graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2019 with an undergraduate degree in math.

My current professional interests lie in the areas of math and computer science, particularly how they work together in the fields of graph theory, game theory, and computer vision.

I grew up in rural central California, and since then have worked to create more STEM educational opportunities accross tradditionally underrepresented communities through the following:

  • Volunteer mentor with MIT's DynaMIT STEM outreach program
  • CS instructor teaching Python to high school students in Israel through MEET, an educational program aimed to create positive social change in the Middle East
  • Guest lecturer teaching knot theory and discrete math at both elementary and higher education schools in the Dominican Republic with the MIT Wind Ensemble
  • Grader for Art of Problem Solving , an online school aimed at introducing middle and high school students to non-traditional areas of math, regardless of geographic location

My interests also include hiking, music, and literature. I concentrated in literature during my undergrad at MIT and enjoy discovering lesser-known poets such as Samuel Hoffenstein. I also currently play trombone in the MIT Wind Ensemble, which I have been a member of since 2015.

I support and believe in Federico Ardila's axioms:

  • Mathematical talent is distributed equally among different groups, irrespective of geographic, demographic, and economic boundaries.
  • Everyone can have joyful, meaningful, and empowering mathematical experiences.
  • Mathematics is a powerful, malleable tool that can be shaped and used differently by communities to serve their needs.
  • Every student deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.
